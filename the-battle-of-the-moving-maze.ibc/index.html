The Battle of the Moving Maze

By: Arlo

Prologue: A Dark Prophecy

From the ashes one shall rise

A merciless king who kills all the despise

But only 1 child can stop it

A decision that will save the world or drop it

Chapter 1: Fitzgerald Primary

Fitzgerald Primary was one of the worst and most boring schools in Australia. Everything in the school was grey. The walls were grey, the uniforms were grey, even the students’ moods were grey! Our story begins in a year 6 class, specifically a year six boy who had no idea of the events to come in the next 48 hours.

“Lunchtime everyone.” said the teacher, Mr. Crack, in a voice that sounded like a drone.

At most schools lunchtime was a break from classes. A way to clear the mind. Fitzgerald Primary was not like most schools. The only food that the school allowed were cheese sandwiches with wholemeal bread. The students grumbled and ate in complete silence. That was Fitzgerald Primary in a nutcase. Boring classes, boring lunch, boring teachers.

At the end of the day, Craig always walked home with an exaggerated slouch. Only one more year at that dump until he was off to high school.

He turned onto his street. If only something interesting would happen to him. All he did all day was school, homework, dinner and sleep. It was the same cycle every day. On weekends, all he did was read. His parents didn’t believe in screens. Because of this, Craig was actually quite smart but there wasn’t an extended learning program at Fitzgerald Primary.

He got out his house keys and jiggled them around in the keyhole until the front door swung open. Craig was always the first one home. Both of his parents worked into the evening and weren’t home until about 7pm. Craig sat down at his desk and got out his laptop. His english homework was a 1000 word essay on the importance of character development in Shakespeare’s Macbeth. Craig sighed. Did he really have to do this? He remembered the school motto. ‘Shut up and do it’. Craig started typing.

He was about 150 words or so into the essay, when he heard a scraping and tapping noise coming from his wardrobe. Must be nothing, he thought and went back to his essay. But the noise continued and it became so annoying that he couldn’t think of what to write in his essay. He got up and opened the door to the wardrobe. Nothing. He stepped forward to have a closer look and fell.

Chapter 2: The Courtyard

Craig’s head throbbed with pain. He slowly opened his eyes. He saw the faint outline of someone staring down at him.

“Oh good, you’re finally awake.”

Craig jolted. A face stared down at him.

“Don’t panic. You’re perfectly safe here.”

Craig focused his eyes on the face in front of him. He looked like a 22 year old male with spiky black hair and wearing a quiver of arrows strapped to his back.

Questions flooded out of Craig like a tsunami, “WhoareyouwhereamIwhatwasinmywadrobethisplacelooksstupidam IreallysafeareyouamadscientistdoIhavesomekindofmindcontrol powerandyouwanttoharnessittotakecontroloftheuniverse?”

“Slow down there buddy, I’m getting to it.”

Craig listened.

“Every year, one 12-year-old comes to this place we call the courtyard. The courtyard becomes a maze every night. Nobody knows how. Every 2nd day we try and solve the clues. In the maze every 100 metres or so there are padlocks on impenetrable doors. On the door is 1 clue about what the code is. If we fail a padlock more than 3 times then the scars, armoured unkillable demons armour, take 5 people and we try again. We have to communicate our thinking. But here’s the catch, if we make a single sound, the scars kill us instantly. Scars are armoured creatures that come up out of the ground. They hold flaming hot swords that can cut through your head off like a hot knife through butter. You talk, and they move faster than the speed of light and cut your head clean off. You kinda have to see it to believe it”

The courtyard was split into 4 parts, the kitchen, the bunks, the weapons shed and the forest. The man (named Trent) seemed to be one of the nice ones. Because of the system, no two boys were the same age. The oldest person in the courtyard was an eagle eyed man named Hex aged 56.

Trent gave Craig a bit of a tour and gave him some weapons. Craig pointed out that surely weapons would be useless if what he heard about the scars were true, but Trent said they were handy against pests and also good for getting food.

Soon came the night and Craig was incredibly nervous. Trent had given him strict instructions to stay right behind him and not to say ANYTHING. The men of the courtyard sat around the fire eating dead eagle and rat meat. Craig had noticed that all of the people there were men. Ben, the 13 year old, seemed nice.

“The first time in the maze is always difficult,” Ben had said in a thick Irish accent, “Just don’t talk and you’ll be fine.”

Soon came bedtime. Craig was not only nervous, he was curious. How long would he be here? What about his life back home? Why him? This whole thing was totally weird. He still didn’t entirely believe it himself. He rolled over and drifted off to sleep.

Chapter 3: The Maze

Craig awoke to complete silence. He looked around and almost cried out in shock. The maze was huge. Ivy covered walls had grown out of the earth like pillars. Short grass covered the soft ground. Craig scanned the horizon and eventually located Trent. He jogged over to him and just managed to stop himself from saying hello. Trent put his finger to his mouth, indicating the importance of staying silent. He pointed his finger forward and the group of boys set off jogging into the maze.

THE END
